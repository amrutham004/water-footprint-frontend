About Water Footprint Calculator

Understand your water use – blue, green, and grey – and learn how to reduce it.

1. What is a Water Footprint and What Does It Calculate?

A Water Footprint measures the total freshwater used — both directly and indirectly — to produce goods, provide services, and carry out daily activities.

  • Blue Water: Water taken from rivers, lakes, or underground sources used for drinking, washing, or industrial purposes.
  • Green Water: Rainwater absorbed into soil and used by plants — important in agriculture and natural ecosystems.
  • Grey Water: The volume required to dilute pollutants so water remains safe for use.

2. How is it Helpful and How Does it Measure Water Footprint?

The calculator collects information about your usage (appliances, hot water use, washing habits) and uses established data to estimate total water consumption.

  • Integrates appliance usage patterns (washing machines, showers, dishwashers).
  • Compares your usage to the average person’s footprint.
  • Provides advice on water-efficient practices.
  • Reveals hidden or indirect water that goes into producing goods and food.

3. Precautions to Save Water

  • Fix leaks in taps and pipes immediately.
  • Use washing machines and dishwashers only when full.
  • Install smart meters or sensors to monitor water in real time.
  • Harvest rainwater for gardening or cleaning.
  • Turn off taps when brushing teeth or shaving.
  • Reuse safe water for cleaning or gardening.
  • Spread awareness about water conservation in your community.
